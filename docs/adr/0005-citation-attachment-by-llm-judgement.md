# Citation Attachment by LLM Judgement

When the **Claim Extractor** emits a **Claim**, it also lists the **Citations** the LLM judges support that **Claim** — there is no positional fallback (e.g. "a **Citation** attaches to whichever **Claim**'s span contains it"). The reason a future reader might assume the opposite — "the **Citation** is right there in the text, just attach it to whatever surrounds it" — is that the headline case the **Claim**-as-unit model exists to handle is the shared-context list ("Several pretrained models — including BERT `[@a]`, GPT `[@b]`, and T5 `[@c]` — pretrain on large corpora"), where each **Citation** belongs to a specific sibling **Claim** and a positional rule would collapse all three onto a single composite **Claim**. A positional fallback would also silently paper over Extractor under-attachment bugs (a **Citation** the LLM forgot to attach would still resolve "correctly" by position), which we want visible in golden-set evals rather than hidden. Trade-off accepted: when the Extractor under-attaches, `UncitedClaim` **Findings** will fire on **Claims** whose **Citation** is sitting in plain sight a few words away; the `Confidence` field and Extractor prompt tuning are the mitigations, not a positional rule. **Citations** the Extractor attaches to no **Claim** are still subject to **Source Resolution** — orphan **Citations** can still surface as `UnresolvedCitation` or `FabricatedSource` **Findings**.
